# The Software-Challenge

The Software-Challenge is a project for school students organised by the Institute of Computer Science at Kiel University (CAU)
and funded by numerous companies and the Prof. Dr. Werner Petersen Foundation.
In cooperation with the schools,
the Institute of Computer Science and the companies,
this project aims to help shape computer science education in a practical way and thus enhance it.
The subject of the Software-Challenge is a programming competition that runs throughout the school year
and offers pupils the opportunity to enter the world of computer science with fun and excitement
as well as with expert guidance.

At the end of the development phase,
each team (course or working group) sends a self-programmed [computer player](/grundlagen/player) for the game into the competition.
This competition initially consists of a complete championship (group stage and champions)
and then a final of the 8 leading teams (Final Eight).
In addition to numerous non-cash prizes,
scholarships for the best pupils are also awarded.

## Conditions of participation and registration

All schools in the Federal Republic of Germany that lead to a university entrance qualification 
and all partners in specific programs are eligible to participate.
Each school can register with several courses or working groups (maximum 3 teams).
Each team can consist of an entire class, an individual or a small group.
Each course or working group may only register one team.
In the initial phase,
it can make sense to form several groups within a team to test different strategies
and develop [computer players](/grundlagen/player), for example.
However, by the start of the competition phase at the latest,
the team must agree on one [computer player](/grundlagen/player) that will then be sent into the competition
and possibly developed further together.
In justified individual cases,
the competition management may allow exceptions to the aforementioned rules.

Currently,
about 40 teams from all over Germany take part in the Software-Challenge each school year.
In addition to numerous non-cash prizes,
scholarships for Kiel University (CAU) or Wedel University of Applied Sciences
(12 months of 300 euros each)
are also awarded to pupils recommended by teachers.

Exact dates for the current period can be found on the
[website under Schedule and Dates](https://software-challenge.de/ablauf-und-termine).

To enable good planning and thus a successful process,
we depend on interested headteachers, teachers,
and pupils to register their schools and teams as early as possible
and, if necessary, update their data.

## The task

The Software-Challenge is based each year on a board game
for which a [computer player](/grundlagen/player) is to be written,
and which is either developed independently
or modified on the basis of an existing board game.
In the later case,
we are supported by the game publishers,
who grant us permission and provide graphics.
In developing or modifying the games,
we try to ensure that the games are interesting,
varied,
not too complicated,
and that the element of luck does not dominate.
A very good [computer player](/grundlagen/player) should therefore prevail over weaker players in the long term,
although it may occasionally lose if it is very unlucky.
